The E-Mobility paradox
E-Mobility contributes 21.4% of revenue but 26.8% of emissions — a 5.4 percentage point gap that makes it the most carbon-intensive division per euro earned.
1. Energy-intensive manufacturing at scale-up stage
Battery component and e-drive production requires high-temperature processes (sintering, coating, cell assembly) — energy demand is front-loaded before economies of scale kick in.
2. Revenue still ramping — denominator effect
At €5.0B, E-Mobility is growing fast but hasn't reached the revenue density of mature divisions. Fixed emissions from new factories are spread across lower revenue per plant.
3. Grid carbon intensity at production sites
Scope 2 emissions depend heavily on local electricity grid mix. E-Mobility plants in regions with coal-heavy grids inflate the intensity even if the product itself enables decarbonization.
Implication: intensity will drop as revenue scales
This is a growth-stage effect, not a structural flaw
